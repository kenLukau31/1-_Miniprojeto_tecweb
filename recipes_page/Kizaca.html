30 min

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried hominy corn
  • 1 cup dried red kidney beans
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 lb chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1/2 lb smoked sausage or linguica, sliced
  • 1/2 lb pork, diced
  • 1/2 lb beef, diced
  • 1/2 lb fish, diced (optional)
  • 1/2 lb cabbage, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 potatoes, diced
  • 2 plantains, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Hominy and Beans:
    • Rinse the hominy and beans separately under cold water
    • Soak them overnight in separate bowls with enough water to cover
  2. Cook the Hominy:
    • In a large pot, add the hominy with fresh water.
    • Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to simmer.
    • Cook until the hominy is tender, which may take a few hours.
  3. Cook the Beans:
    • In another pot, add the soaked kidney beans with fresh water
    • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the beans are tender.
  4. Prepare the Meats:
    • In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onions, minced garlic, and bay leaves. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
    • Add the chorizo, smoked sausage, pork, beef, and fish. Cook until the meats are browned.
  5. Combine and Cook:
    • Add the cooked hominy and kidney beans to the pot with the meats.
    • Add enough water to cover the mixture.
    • Add the chopped cabbage, diced carrots, diced potatoes, and sliced plantains.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until all the ingredients are cooked and the flavors meld together.
  6. Serve:
    • Remove the bay leaves before serving.
    • Cachupa is often served with a side of hot sauce or a spicy pepper paste.
